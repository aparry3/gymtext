Score each dimension 0–10. Use weights to compute weighted total.

Gating rules (automatic major penalty):
- If the output asks the user questions or includes “assumptions/next steps”: cap total at 6/10.
- If it outputs a week-by-week plan, long exercise lists, or highly technical load tables without being required by program type: cap total at 7/10.
- If it is clearly not personalized to the profile: cap total at 5/10.

1) Personal Fit & Constraint Respect (Weight 0.35)
10: Built specifically for this user; days/week, session length, equipment, preferences, injuries, and lifestyle constraints are all reflected correctly.
8–9: Strong fit; one minor mismatch (e.g., day ordering slightly off, one constraint not carried into weekly pattern).
5–7: Generally reasonable but could apply to many users; weak integration of preferences/constraints.
3–4: Poor match (wrong frequency, ignores key injury/equipment limits, mismatched goal type).
0–2: Fundamentally inappropriate or unsafe relative to profile.

Evaluate:
- Weekly frequency and recovery match availability
- Equipment reality is honored
- Injury/risk constraints are acknowledged and meaningfully integrated
- Goal alignment (strength vs hypertrophy vs endurance vs general fitness vs rehab)
- Personal preferences (liked/disliked modalities) materially shape the plan

2) Structure Quality & Session Purpose (Weight 0.25)
10: Weekly Pattern is crisp; each day has a distinct purpose and clearly supports the program goal; no redundancy.
8–9: Clear structure with minor vagueness in one session purpose.
5–7: Structure exists but purposes are generic or repetitive; unclear why days are arranged as they are.
3–4: Disorganized; days don’t connect to goals; missing rest/recovery logic.
0–2: No meaningful structure.

Evaluate:
- Day-by-day purpose is explicit
- Movement patterns / energy systems are appropriately balanced
- Rest days and optional recovery are appropriate to user and goal
- Not overly prescriptive (see Dimension 4)

3) Progression & Adaptation Strategy (Weight 0.20)
10: Simple, correct progression logic that matches modality and experience; includes readiness adjustment and recovery strategy when appropriate.
8–9: Solid but one element unclear (e.g., cutback timing not stated).
5–7: Vague (“progress over time”) without actionable rules.
3–4: Progression is inappropriate to the user (too complex for novice, too aggressive for rehab, etc.).
0–2: No progression strategy or nonsensical guidance.

Evaluate:
- Progression matches goal type (strength, hypertrophy, endurance, rehab, general)
- Complexity matches training age
- Adaptation rules are simple and usable (e.g., “if sleep is poor and soreness high, reduce volume by ~20%”)
- Recovery logic is included only when needed and described plainly

4) Right Level of Specificity (Not Over-Prescriptive) (Weight 0.10)
10: Correct “program-level” detail—focuses, intents, ranges, anchor movements only when needed; avoids full exercise menus and exact prescriptions.
8–9: Slightly too specific in one section but still mostly program-level.
5–7: Frequently drifts into weekly-plan territory (many exercises, sets/reps, pace tables) without necessity.
3–4: Overly prescriptive throughout or oddly vague (“do stuff”).
0–2: Essentially a full weekly plan, spreadsheet, or technical template—wrong artifact type.

Evaluate:
- Uses ranges and intent rather than exact loads everywhere
- Names exact exercises only when essential (safety/goal specificity)
- Avoids long lists; stays dossier-level

5) Clarity, Concision & Dossier Compliance (Weight 0.10)
10: Clean headings, scannable bullets, concise language; exactly matches required output format; reads like a coach’s plan.
8–9: Clear but slightly wordy or minor formatting drift.
5–7: Understandable but verbose, repetitive, or missing a required section.
3–4: Hard to follow; poor formatting; multiple missing sections.
0–2: Unclear, inconsistent, or noncompliant.

Evaluate:
- Required headings present and in order
- No extraneous sections (no Q&A, no “notes to user”)
- Minimal jargon; plain language
- Concise while still covering essentials
